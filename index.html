About Me

I am particularly interested in how communication and education influence health outcomes. This perspective has guided my involvement in initiatives such as KiDES, where I have worked to develop accessible, developmentally appropriate approaches to teaching anatomical concepts to children in clinical and educational settings.

My long-term goal is to pursue graduate training in Physiotherapy, where I hope to further integrate clinical expertise with evidence-based practice. I am especially drawn to approaches that enhance patient involvement and understanding, thus supporting meaningful engagement in care.

I value interdisciplinary collaboration, thoughtful problem solving, and the application of research to real-world contexts. I am motivated to contribute to healthcare environments that prioritize both scientific rigor and patient experience.
